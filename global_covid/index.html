A global perspective

Can we see the air pollution - corona correlation on a global level?

The incidence and death rate of COVID-19 has varied significantly between states, regions and even localities. Some factors that explain these differences have been known very early into the pandemic: The age distribution of populations, mobility, population density or medical preconditions such as diabetes or obesity.

Medical professionals and scientists have been researching whether there are also contributing environmental factors that cause COVID19 to spread faster or pose a higher risk to specific populations.New research coming out of that effort in 2020 indicates that air pollution could contribute to higher numbers of COVID19 deaths.
